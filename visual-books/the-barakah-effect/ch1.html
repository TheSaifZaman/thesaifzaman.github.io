What is Barakah?

~25 min read Core Concept

1

Defining Barakah: A Web of Meaning

Barakah is often translated as "blessing," yet this single word fails to capture its profound meaning. The author describes it as an enigmatic concept -- like trying to describe the taste of honey to someone who has never tried it. To truly understand Barakah, we must construct a web of meaning from multiple scholarly definitions.

Imam Al-Raghib Al-Asfahani, an 11th-century scholar, defined Barakah as "the attachment of Divine Goodness to a thing." The 13th-century scholar Ibn Al-Qayyim called it "abundance and permanence of goodness." Dr. Samir Mahmoud described it as "a spiritual energy emanating from Allah that enters people, places, and objects, transforming them into conduits for this divine energy." Dr. Tamara Gray referred to it as "a spiritual force that brings increase to a person's life without a defined input."

Dr. Abdallah Rothman characterised Barakah as "an abundant flowing positive energy resulting from alignment with Allah," while Dr. Khalil Abdur-Rashid of Harvard Divinity School identified it as "a mystical experience -- a form of communication from Allah." Each of these definitions illuminates a different facet of this multifaceted concept.

2

The Four Parts of Barakah

From these scholarly definitions, the author synthesises a comprehensive understanding of Barakah that can be divided into four interconnected parts. These four parts form the foundation for understanding how Barakah operates in our lives.

The Four Parts of Barakah

Barakah is from Allah, placed in things, creates a multiplier effect, and ultimately brings you closer to the Divine.

1. The Divine Nature of Barakah: Barakah is from Allah alone. It is a gift from the Divine to whomever and whatever He wills. Allah described Himself as the source of Barakah in the Quran: "Blessed is the One in Whose Hands rests all authority. And He is Most Capable of everything" (Quran 67:1). Ibn Al-Qayyim commented that all Barakah belongs to Allah and whoever He bestows His Barakah on is blessed.

2. The Divine Placement of Barakah: Allah infuses His Barakah within various aspects of our world -- places, people, time, the Quran, and faith itself. Barakah can attach to anything tangible or intangible: your time, wealth, children, devices, home, relationships, or career. These things become conduits of Barakah, whether or not we recognise their presence.

3

The Barakah Effect & Returning to Allah

3. The Barakah Effect: When Barakah enters something, a spiritual multiplier effect takes place that brings superabundance, prosperity, positivity, happiness, permanence, and continuity. For example, if you have ever been to the Prophet's Mosque in Medina, you will have experienced a special feeling of tranquillity -- a consequence of the Prophet's supplication: "O Allah! Bestow on Medina twice the Barakah You bestowed on Mecca" (Bukhari).

Barakah transcends logical explanation because it exists beyond the cause-and-effect realm. We often struggle to accept that Allah, the Creator of the laws governing cause and effect, can alter these laws as He pleases. We may donate to charity believing our money is lost, yet the Barakah in our remaining funds amplifies their value beyond what was given away.

4. Barakah Leads You Back to Allah: The Barakah you encounter in life should ultimately bring you back to the Bestower of Barakah, Allah. If the blessings you enjoy take you away from Allah, then there is no true Barakah in them. Someone who purchases a car and uses it to serve their community experiences increasing Barakah; someone who uses the same car to disobey Allah may lose whatever Barakah it had.

4

The B Coefficient: A Mathematical Illustration

The author introduces a powerful equation to illustrate Barakah mathematically. To be productive and achieve anything in life, you need three components: Energy x Focus x Time = Outcome. As humans, our energy, focus, and time are finite, so our outcomes are inherently limited.

Now add the Barakah element: B(Energy) x B(Focus) x B(Time) = B(Outcome). Here, Barakah is represented by the B coefficient -- the gift from Allah that enhances your energy, focus, and time. It creates a spiritual multiplier effect that impacts your physical reality.

Example: Say you decide to attract Barakah to your book project by waking up early -- a practice known to bring Barakah to your time. You will likely notice: (1) More energy and motivation to write during the early hours, (2) You are more likely to enter "the zone" and develop original insights, (3) Your 30-minute writing session might produce the equivalent of 2-3 hours of afternoon writing.
5

Physical Manifestations of Barakah

A common perception of Barakah is observing material expansion or growth in areas like business, family, wealth, or time that surpasses expectations and defies logical explanation. The story of Al-Baik is a perfect example: Shakour AbuGhazalah committed to donating one riyal per chicken sold, and 20 years later his restaurant secured an exclusive contract to serve millions of pilgrims in Mina during Hajj.

But not all material manifestations of Barakah appear as increase or growth. Sometimes Barakah manifests as stability -- owning a house for a long period, a lasting marriage, or a stable career. As Ibn Al-Qayyim said, "The reality of Barakah is steadfastness, constancy, and stability." At other times, Barakah manifests as continuity -- small, consistent drops of blessing that compound over time, like neighbours who spend 10 minutes daily reading Quran together and, over 10 years, complete countless books of tafsir.

There are three ways to gauge the physical manifestation of Barakah: (1) Witnessing material growth beyond your expectations and efforts; (2) Observing stability in your affairs such as marriage, income, career, and children; (3) Experiencing continuity in your activities -- projects that may not be growing exponentially but continue consistently.

6

Unseen Manifestations of Barakah

Barakah may also reveal itself in intangible, psychological, and spiritual ways. You might maintain energy and focus on a project even when faced with challenges. You might experience a deep sense of inner peace and tranquillity despite significant shocks or traumas. You might find ease and facilitation in your affairs, even when the situation seems difficult.

Recognising unseen manifestations is crucial because someone may not display physical signs of Barakah -- perhaps due to poverty or unfortunate circumstances -- yet still experience intangible manifestations that make their life feel blessed. They may have inner peace, contentment, and restful sleep at night. The story of the 99 gold coins illustrates this powerfully: a poor, happy servant becomes distressed after receiving 99 coins labelled "100" because the missing one robs him of his contentment.

Two Forms of Barakah
Physical
Growth, Stability, Continuity in wealth, business, family
Unseen
Inner peace, Focus, Ease, Non-material rizq, Contentment
7

Can Non-Muslims Experience Barakah?

A common question is whether non-Muslims can experience Barakah. Based on research and consultation with various scholars, the answer is yes -- non-Muslims can experience Barakah, though they may not fully experience its complete effect as it might not lead them back to the Bestower of Barakah, Allah (unless Allah guides them).

People from all backgrounds can experience the Barakah of waking up early, being honest in business, or giving to charity. The concept of Barakah is an excellent conversation starter with non-Muslim friends and colleagues, especially with People of the Book (Jews and Christians) who believe in God and the Hereafter. It could be a great topic for inter-faith dialogue and a wonderful way to introduce people to the reality of a spiritual life.

Chapter 1 Summary

  • Barakah is from Allah: It is a divine gift that can be placed in anything -- people, places, time, wealth, relationships -- transforming them into conduits of divine energy.
  • Four parts: (1) Divine Nature, (2) Divine Placement, (3) The Barakah Effect (spiritual multiplier), (4) Leading back to Allah.
  • The B Coefficient: B(Energy) x B(Focus) x B(Time) = B(Outcome) -- Barakah acts as a multiplier on all your resources.
  • Physical manifestations: Material growth, stability in affairs, and continuity of effort -- all beyond logical expectations.
  • Unseen manifestations: Inner peace, focus during hardship, ease in affairs, beneficial ideas, and contentment.
  • More with less: Something little with Barakah is better than something abundant without it.
  • 99 Gold Coins: Material wealth without contentment strips away Barakah -- the servant lost his happiness chasing the missing coin.
  • Universal concept: Non-Muslims can experience some forms of Barakah, making it an excellent bridge for inter-faith dialogue.
  • Stick to Barakah: Umar ibn Al-Khattab advised: "Whoever sees Barakah in something, let him stick to it."

Key Terms

Mubarak
Blessed -- one who has received Allah's Barakah.
B Coefficient
The Barakah multiplier that enhances energy, focus, time, and outcomes.
Rizq
Sustenance from Allah -- can be material or non-material (ideas, peace, support).
Barakal Jamal
Arabic expression meaning "the camel knelt and stayed" -- symbolising stability.
Barakat Al-Sama
Arabic for "rain fell continuously" -- symbolising continuity of blessing.
