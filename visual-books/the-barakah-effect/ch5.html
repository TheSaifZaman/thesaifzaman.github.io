Bismillah

~18 min read Manifesto Element

1

The Gateway to Barakah

"Bismillah ir-Rahman ir-Rahim" -- In the Name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. These are the first words of the Quran, opening every chapter except Surah At-Tawbah. They are the first words taught to Muslim children. They are the words that the angel Jibreel spoke to the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) in the cave of Hira when revelation began.

The Gateway to Barakah

Bismillah is the gateway that transforms every ordinary action into an act of worship, inviting divine blessing.

Bismillah is the gateway to Barakah because it sets the spiritual tone for every action that follows it. When you say Bismillah before an activity, you are making a conscious declaration that this action is being done in Allah's name, with His permission, and for His sake. You are inviting the divine into the mundane.

The author describes Bismillah as the first and most fundamental element of the Barakah Culture Manifesto. If you practise nothing else from this book, practise this. It costs nothing, takes less than a second, and transforms every ordinary action into an act of worship.

The Basmala -- Word by Word

The Basmala encodes an entire worldview: dependence on Allah, acknowledgement of His sovereignty, and trust in His encompassing and specific mercy.

A Profound Simplicity: Bismillah is perhaps the simplest spiritual practice in existence -- three words that take less than two seconds to say. Yet scholars have written entire volumes on its meaning. Its simplicity is its power: it can be practised by anyone, anywhere, at any time, before any action.
2

The Power of Beginning with Allah's Name

The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) emphasised saying Bismillah in numerous hadiths, establishing it as a Sunnah before virtually every activity. Before eating: "Mention the Name of Allah and eat with your right hand" (Bukhari and Muslim). Before entering the home: the Prophet taught that saying Bismillah when entering keeps Shaytan away (Muslim). Before wudu: "There is no wudu for the one who does not mention Allah's Name over it" (Abu Dawud).

The Three Functions of Bismillah

Bismillah simultaneously acknowledges Allah's sovereignty, requests His help, and invokes His protection.

Each Bismillah is a micro-reset -- a momentary pause that reconnects you to your Creator before proceeding with the task at hand. In a world of distraction, where we move from one task to the next on autopilot, Bismillah forces a moment of presence. It is mindfulness rooted in tawhid (the oneness of Allah).

The scholars explain that Bismillah serves three functions simultaneously: (1) It is an acknowledgement that all ability comes from Allah. (2) It is a request for Allah's help and blessing. (3) It is a protection against harm, as Shaytan flees from the remembrance of Allah.

The Bismillah Micro-Reset Cycle

Bismillah breaks the autopilot cycle: pause, reconnect with Allah, then act with conscious purpose.

Bismillah in Daily Life

The beauty of Bismillah is its universality -- it applies to every lawful activity. The author encourages readers to audit their daily routine and identify every moment where Bismillah can be said. The result is a day saturated with divine remembrance.

Bismillah Throughout Your Day

Bismillah radiates into every activity of the day -- transforming ordinary tasks into moments of divine connection and invitations for Barakah.

Before meals: Saying Bismillah before eating invites Barakah into your food. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said that when someone forgets and remembers mid-meal, they should say "Bismillah awwalahu wa akhirahu" (In the Name of Allah, at its beginning and end).

Before work: Whether you are opening your laptop, entering a meeting, or starting a project, Bismillah transforms professional activity into worship. It shifts your intention from "earning a salary" to "serving Allah through my skills."

Before travel: The Prophet (peace be upon him) taught specific du'as for travel that begin with Bismillah. Every journey, from a long flight to a short drive, becomes an opportunity for Barakah.

Before writing and studying: Scholars have always begun their works with Bismillah. When you open a textbook, start an exam, or write an email, Bismillah invites clarity and Barakah into your intellectual efforts.

Before sleep: Saying Bismillah before sleeping places you under Allah's protection through the night, and you wake refreshed with the Barakah of a restful sleep.

The Ripple Effect of One Bismillah

One Bismillah creates ripples that extend far beyond the single action -- purifying intent, inviting blessing, and transforming your entire day.

When We Forget Bismillah

What happens when Bismillah is absent from our actions? The author argues that activities performed without Bismillah become purely worldly -- stripped of their spiritual dimension. The Prophet (peace be upon him) indicated that a matter of importance not begun with Bismillah is "abtar" -- cut off from blessing.

With vs. Without Bismillah

Actions with Bismillah bear fruit in both worlds; without it, they are "abtar" -- severed from divine blessing.

This does not mean that forgetting Bismillah once ruins your entire day. Allah is merciful and forgiving. But a pattern of omitting Bismillah reflects a deeper spiritual issue: a heart that has become heedless (ghafil) of Allah's presence in daily life.

The consequence of systematic Bismillah-absence is that your actions become compartmentalised: "religious" activities (prayer, Quran) are seen as separate from "worldly" activities (work, eating, commuting). This compartmentalisation is precisely what hustle culture wants -- it relegates God to a corner of your life rather than the centre.

The Danger of Compartmentalisation

Without Bismillah, life splits into "religious" and "worldly" compartments. With Bismillah, Allah is at the centre of everything.

The Heedlessness Spectrum

Heedlessness is a spectrum. Even moving from "occasional forgetfulness" to "growing awareness" brings measurable Barakah.

The Remedy: If you have been living without Bismillah, start small. Pick three activities today -- one meal, one work task, one act of entering your home -- and consciously say Bismillah before each. Tomorrow, add three more. Within a week, Bismillah will become second nature, and you will begin to notice the Barakah it brings.

Bismillah is not just a formula or a superstition. It is a worldview compressed into three words: everything begins with Allah, depends on Allah, and returns to Allah. When you truly internalise this, every moment of your life becomes a gateway to Barakah.

The Bismillah Worldview

Bismillah encapsulates the believer's entire worldview: everything begins with Allah, depends on Allah, and returns to Allah.

Chapter 5 Summary

  • First manifesto element: Bismillah is the gateway to Barakah -- the simplest yet most profound spiritual practice, taking less than two seconds.
  • Opens every surah: Bismillah opens every chapter of the Quran except At-Tawbah. It is the first thing taught to Muslim children.
  • Three functions: Acknowledgement (all ability from Allah), Request (for His help and blessing), Protection (Shaytan flees from Allah's remembrance).
  • Prophetic emphasis: "Any matter of importance not begun with Bismillah is cut off from blessing." The Prophet taught Bismillah before eating, entering the home, wudu, and more.
  • Universal application: Bismillah applies to every lawful activity -- meals, work, travel, study, sleep, meetings, writing.
  • When absent: Actions become purely worldly, stripped of spiritual dimension. Systematic omission reflects heedlessness (ghaflah) and compartmentalises religion away from daily life.
  • Start small: Pick three activities today and say Bismillah before each. Expand daily until it becomes second nature.
