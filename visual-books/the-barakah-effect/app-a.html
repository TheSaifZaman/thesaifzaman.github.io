Appendix A

21 Sources of Barakah

~25 min read Reference Guide

1

Spiritual Sources of Barakah

The most powerful sources of Barakah are those that connect you directly to Allah. These seven spiritual sources form the inner core of a blessed life -- they are the foundation upon which all other sources rest. Without these, the remaining sources lose their potency.

1. The Quran: Reading, memorising, and implementing the Quran is the greatest source of Barakah. Allah describes the Quran as "a blessed Book which We have revealed to you" (Quran 38:29). The Quran brings Barakah to your time, heart, mind, and home. Households where the Quran is recited regularly are described as having light that is visible to the inhabitants of the heavens.

2. Salah (Prayer): Establishing the five daily prayers, especially in congregation, is a tremendous source of Barakah. Prayer in congregation is 27 times more rewarding than prayer alone. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said that the congregational prayer purifies and blesses the time around it.

3. Du'a (Supplication): Asking Allah directly for Barakah in your affairs is itself a source of Barakah. The Prophet (peace be upon him) would frequently ask Allah for Barakah in his food, time, family, and work. Du'a demonstrates your dependence on Allah and opens the doors of divine provision.

4. Dhikr (Remembrance of Allah): Keeping your tongue moist with the remembrance of Allah brings tranquillity and Barakah to your heart and life. "Verily, in the remembrance of Allah do hearts find rest" (Quran 13:28). Morning and evening adhkar are a protective shield that attracts Barakah throughout the day.

5. Bismillah (Starting with Allah's Name): Beginning every action with "Bismillah" (In the name of Allah) invites Barakah into that action. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said that any matter of importance not begun with Bismillah is cut off from Barakah. Whether eating, entering your home, or starting a project -- Bismillah is the key.

6. Tawbah (Repentance): Sincere repentance removes the spiritual blockages that prevent Barakah from entering your life. Allah says: "Ask forgiveness of your Lord. Indeed, He is ever a Perpetual Forgiver. He will send rain to you in abundance and give you increase in wealth and children and provide for you gardens and provide for you rivers" (Quran 71:10-12). Istighfar (seeking forgiveness) is directly linked to material and spiritual provision.

7. Taqwa (God-Consciousness): Living with an awareness of Allah in all your affairs brings Barakah from sources you never imagined. "And whoever fears Allah -- He will make for him a way out and will provide for him from where he does not expect" (Quran 65:2-3). Taqwa is the overarching quality that ties all spiritual sources together.

Seven Spiritual Sources of Barakah

The seven spiritual sources form the inner ring of Barakah -- they connect you directly to Allah, the ultimate source of all blessing.

2

Character & Relationship Sources

Beyond your personal connection with Allah, the way you treat other people is a major gateway for Barakah. The Prophet (peace be upon him) emphasised that the best among people are those best in character and most beneficial to others. These six sources focus on your relationships and conduct.

8. Honouring Parents (Birr al-Walidayn): Treating your parents with mercy, compassion, and service is one of the most powerful sources of Barakah. Allah pairs obedience to parents with obedience to Himself in the Quran. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "The pleasure of the Lord lies in the pleasure of the parent, and the displeasure of the Lord lies in the displeasure of the parent." A person who serves their parents well often finds doors of provision opening from unexpected places.

9. Maintaining Family Ties (Silat al-Rahim): Keeping ties with relatives -- even those who are difficult -- is a source of increased provision and extended life. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "Whoever would like his provision to be increased and his lifespan to be extended, let him maintain his family ties." Cutting family ties is one of the surest ways to lose Barakah.

10. Truthfulness and Honesty: Being truthful in business, speech, and dealings attracts Barakah, while dishonesty destroys it. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said about two parties in a transaction: "If they are truthful and transparent, their transaction will be blessed. But if they lie and conceal, the blessing of their transaction will be erased."

11. Good Character (Husn al-Khuluq): A beautiful character -- patience, gentleness, generosity, humility -- draws people towards you and opens doors of goodness. The Prophet (peace be upon him) was asked about what admits most people to Paradise and he said: "Taqwa of Allah and good character." Good character is a magnet for Barakah in all your relationships.

12. Being Just and Fair: Justice in all your dealings -- with employees, family, neighbours, and even enemies -- is a source of divine Barakah. Allah says: "Indeed, Allah orders justice and good conduct" (Quran 16:90). The just ruler will be shaded on the Day of Judgement, and justice in this world brings stability and trust -- both manifestations of Barakah.

13. Showing Mercy and Compassion: The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "The merciful are shown mercy by the Most Merciful. Be merciful on the earth, and you will be shown mercy from above." Compassion towards creation -- people, animals, the environment -- unlocks divine mercy and Barakah in your life.

Relationship Web of Barakah

Your character sits at the centre -- each relationship you nurture with honesty, justice, and mercy becomes a channel of Barakah.

3

Financial & Material Sources

How you earn, spend, and share your wealth directly impacts the Barakah in your financial life. Islam provides a comprehensive system for ensuring that money flows with Barakah rather than becoming a source of anxiety and spiritual harm.

14. Halal Income: Earning through permissible means is the foundation of financial Barakah. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "No one has ever eaten food better than that which he earned by working with his own hands." Haram income -- no matter how abundant -- is stripped of Barakah and eventually brings ruin. A small halal income with Barakah is worth more than a large haram fortune.

15. Giving Charity (Sadaqah): Giving in the way of Allah does not decrease wealth; it multiplies it. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "Charity does not diminish wealth." This is the Barakah paradox -- the more you give, the more you receive. Sadaqah can be financial, but it also includes a kind word, removing harm from the road, or even a smile.

16. Paying Zakat: The obligatory charity of Zakat purifies your wealth and attracts Barakah to what remains. The word "Zakat" itself comes from a root meaning "purification and growth." By paying Zakat, you acknowledge that your wealth belongs to Allah and that a portion is owed to the less fortunate. This purification allows the remaining wealth to grow with Barakah.

17. Sharing Food: The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "The food of one is enough for two, and the food of two is enough for four, and the food of four is enough for eight." Sharing food is a simple yet profound source of Barakah. Eating together, inviting others to your table, and being generous with what you have brings Barakah to the food itself.

18. Avoiding Waste (Israf): Extravagance and waste destroy Barakah. Allah says: "Eat and drink, but do not be excessive. Indeed, He does not like those who are excessive" (Quran 7:31). Being mindful of consumption -- not wasting food, water, money, or time -- preserves the Barakah in what you have.

19. Contentment (Qana'ah): Being content with what Allah has given you is a source of Barakah in itself. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "Richness is not having many possessions, but richness is the richness of the soul." Contentment does not mean lack of ambition; it means trusting that what Allah has given you right now is sufficient, while still striving for more through blessed means.

Financial Barakah Flow

Halal earnings flow through acts of giving -- Zakat, Sadaqah, sharing food -- and Barakah returns to multiply your wealth.

4

Time & Lifestyle Sources

The final group of sources relates to how you structure your day, follow the Prophetic lifestyle, and engage with your community. These sources bring Barakah into the very fabric of your daily routine.

20. Waking Up Early: The early hours of the morning carry special Barakah. The Prophet (peace be upon him) made a beautiful supplication for his Ummah: "O Allah, put Barakah for my nation in the early hours." Successful scholars, entrepreneurs, and leaders throughout Islamic history rose before Fajr to benefit from this blessed time. The hours between Fajr and sunrise are described as among the most productive and blessed periods of the day.

21. Following the Sunnah: The Prophetic way of life -- from how you eat, sleep, and interact, to how you conduct business and worship -- is a comprehensive source of Barakah. The Sunnah represents the most blessed way to live because it was the way of the most blessed human being to have ever walked the earth. Every Sunnah you implement, no matter how small, adds a layer of Barakah to your life.

Additional lifestyle sources include: Suhoor (the pre-dawn meal) -- the Prophet (peace be upon him) said "Take Suhoor, for in Suhoor there is Barakah"; Gathering for knowledge -- circles of learning attract angels and Barakah; and Making Hijrah for Allah's sake -- leaving what is harmful for what is blessed, whether that is a physical move or a spiritual transformation.

Hadith: The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "O Allah, put Barakah for my nation in the early hours." (Narrated by Sakhr al-Ghamidi, reported in Sunan Abu Dawud and Tirmidhi). This du'a is specifically for the Muslim Ummah, highlighting that the early hours carry a special divine blessing for those who rise to benefit from them.

Appendix A Summary

  • Spiritual Sources (1-7): The Quran, Salah (especially in congregation), Du'a, Dhikr, Bismillah, Tawbah, and Taqwa form the inner ring of Barakah -- the direct connection to Allah.
  • Character & Relationship Sources (8-13): Honouring parents, maintaining family ties, truthfulness, good character, justice, and mercy -- how you treat others determines how much Barakah flows through your relationships.
  • Financial Sources (14-19): Halal income, Sadaqah, Zakat, sharing food, avoiding waste, and contentment -- the way you earn, spend, and share determines the Barakah in your wealth.
  • Time & Lifestyle Sources (20-21): Waking up early and following the Sunnah, along with Suhoor, seeking knowledge, and making Hijrah -- structuring your life around blessed practices.
  • Key principle: These 21 sources are not a checklist to complete overnight -- they are a lifelong journey. Start with what is most accessible and gradually build your capacity for attracting Barakah.
  • Interconnected: The sources reinforce each other. Taqwa leads to truthfulness, which strengthens family ties, which opens doors of provision, which enables more Sadaqah -- a virtuous cycle of Barakah.
