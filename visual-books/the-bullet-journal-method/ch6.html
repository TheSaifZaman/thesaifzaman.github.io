The Monthly Ritual

What is Migration?

Migration is the monthly process of filtering out meaningless content from your notebook. At the end of each month, review your entries and ask: Is this still important? Is it still relevant?

Migration is the cornerstone of the Bullet Journal method. It forces regular reflection, ensuring your notebook only contains things that matter. It is your built-in self-awareness checkpoint.
The Migration decision flow: review, filter, move forward

Types of Migration

>
Monthly Migration
Move open tasks from last month into the new Monthly Log. Happens at the end of each month.
<
Scheduling
Move tasks tied to a specific future date into the Future Log. The "<" points backward to the front of the notebook.
✗
Yearly Migration
When starting a new notebook, review everything in the old one. Only migrate what truly matters into the new volume.

Chapter 6 Summary

Migration

The monthly process of reviewing open tasks and deciding: still important? Cross it off, migrate it forward (>), or schedule it (<) to the Future Log. Forces regular self-reflection.

Three Types

Monthly Migration (end of month to new month), Scheduling (to Future Log), and Yearly Migration (old notebook to new notebook).

Setup Steps

1. Number pages. 2. Create Index (pages 1-4). 3. Future Log (5-8). 4. Monthly Log (9-10). 5. Start Daily Log. 6. Update Index.
