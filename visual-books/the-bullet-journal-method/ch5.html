What Are Collections?

Collections are the modular building blocks of BuJo, used to store related content. Think of them like Lego pieces—each serves a specific function, and you can mix and match them to meet your needs.

There are four core Collections. Click each to explore:

The Daily Log

The Daily Log is your workhorse. Each day, write the date as your Topic and then Rapid Log your Tasks, Events, and Notes throughout the day. Don't set up days in advance—just log the new day as the previous one ends.

03.29.SA
✗ Keith: Call re: Saturday dinner
• Acme Co: Release forms
• Heather: Email to get forms
> Email forms to participants
< Get signatures
— Acme Co: UX presentation Feb 12
• Leigh: Reply Apr 21 party
— Office closed Apr 13
◦ Margaret: Volunteered to help with assets
— Showing more incentive

The Monthly Log

The Monthly Log provides a bird's-eye view of your month. It consists of two facing pages:

Calendar Page

List each day of the month with its first letter (1M, 2T, 3W...). Log events and deadlines next to their dates. Keep it lean.

Task Page

A prioritized list of Tasks for the month. Functions as your monthly Mental Inventory. Transfer items from the previous month's Daily Logs.

The Future Log & Index

Future Log: Placed near the front of your notebook, it stores Tasks and Events that fall outside the current month. Typically divided into 6 months across two spreads.

The Index: The first few pages of your notebook. Record Topics and their page numbers as you create them. This makes any content instantly findable.
