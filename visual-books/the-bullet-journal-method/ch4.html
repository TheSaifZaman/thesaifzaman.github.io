What is Rapid Logging?

If Rapid Logging is the language the Bullet Journal is written in, Bullets are the syntax. It is a way to efficiently capture your life as it happens using short-form notation paired with symbols.

Rapid Logging will help you efficiently capture your life as it happens so that you may begin to study it.

Traditional journaling uses 225 words to capture a day. Rapid Logging captures the same information in 89 words—about 60% less!

Topics & Pagination

Every page starts with a Topic (the title) and a page number. Topics serve three functions:

1
Identify
Describe the content
2
Clarify
Set your intention
3
Agenda
Set the page's purpose
Often all it takes to live intentionally is to pause before you proceed. Giving your page a Topic provides that pause.

The Bullet System

Entries are categorized into three types, each with its own symbol. Click each bullet type to learn more:

•
Task
Things you need to do

Tasks have five states:

•Open Task — action required ✗Completed — done! >Migrated — moved forward to next month or collection <Scheduled — moved backward to Future Log •Irrelevant — no longer matters, strike it off
◦
Event
Experience-related entries

Events are represented by the open circle "◦". They can be scheduled preemptively ("Charlie's birthday party") or logged after they occur ("Signed the lease. Yay!").

Key rule: Keep events as objective and brief as possible, no matter how personal or emotional. This makes it more likely you will write them down.

—
Note
Information you don't want to forget

Notes are represented by a dash "—". They capture facts, ideas, thoughts, and observations. Notes can be nested under Tasks or Events as supporting details.

Signifiers & Custom Bullets

Signifiers are symbols placed in front of Bullets to add extra context:

*
Priority
Important tasks to focus on
!
Inspiration
Ideas and insights to revisit

You can create your own custom bullets and signifiers. The system is designed to be extended to fit your unique needs.

Chapter 4 Summary

Rapid Logging

Short-form notation using symbols to quickly capture, categorize, and prioritize thoughts into Tasks, Events, and Notes. Uses 60% fewer words than traditional journaling.

Topics & Pagination

Every page gets a descriptive Topic and page number. Topics identify content, clarify intention, and set the agenda. Page numbers enable the Index to work.
