Chapter 8Goals & Growth

Setting Meaningful Goals

Most goals fail not because we lack willpower, but because they lack meaning. Before setting a goal, ask yourself why it matters. Carroll introduces a goal-setting framework that bridges the gap between dreams and action.

Goals should be based on what you actually believe in, not on what you think you should believe in. A goal rooted in genuine curiosity will outlast one driven by social pressure.

The Goal Sprint System

Carroll adapts the Agile Sprint methodology for personal goals. Break large goals into smaller, time-bound Sprints:

Goals → Sprints → Tasks: Making the ambitious achievable

Small Steps: The Power of Incrementalism

Progress doesn't require giant leaps. Small, consistent steps compound into remarkable results. Carroll describes this as kaizen—the Japanese philosophy of continuous improvement through small changes.

The 1% Rule

If you improve just 1% each day, after one year you'll be 37 times better. The key is consistency over intensity.

Time & Gratitude

Time: We can't manage time; we can only manage how we spend it. BuJo helps by making time visible through logging, so you can spot patterns of waste and opportunity.

Gratitude: Research consistently shows that gratitude practice improves well-being. Carroll suggests adding a gratitude log to your BuJo—even just one thing per day.

Control: What You Can Influence

Carroll draws on Stoic philosophy, particularly the dichotomy of control: focus your energy on what you can control and accept what you cannot.
